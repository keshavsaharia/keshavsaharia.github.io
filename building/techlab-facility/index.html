TechLab facility

When I started my first business, I converted a run-down commercial space into a modern environment for technology classes.

In 2013, I started TechLab Education, an education space in Saratoga. The facility has evolved considerably over the last three years, and it is now one of the most advanced makerspaces in the Bay Area. We host dozens of classes year round, and rent our facility out to other organizations looking to offer classes and meetups in the South Bay Area. You can contact operations@techlabeducation.com to inquire about our Saratoga location.

This post is a quick overview at how the facility has changed over time, and the most exciting use cases we've had.

The front room

When I got the space, it was a run-down commercial location overrun by dust and spiderwebs. The first thing I did was paint the walls white and install new floors in the front room. I wanted the front to feel like a showroom for our classes and technology.

The front room is where we do most of our robotics and afterschool classes for younger kids. I installed directed lighting throughout, and every single piece of furniture is from Ikea.

The back room

The back room has a more classroom-like feel. It is where we do our high school and adult classes, and comfortably seats 24 students. It also has the directed lighting and the same projector/TV installations as the front room. I installed the carpeting myself, which is why it began falling apart after 2 years and at the time of this writing needs to be replaced. It was a valuable life lesson - leave carpet installation to the professionals.

What I learned

Before building the basement, I didn't have much experience working with my hands aside from a middle school woodshop class. After building the techlab facility, I added the same IoT systems I had built in the basement like voice controlled lights and intelligent appliances.
